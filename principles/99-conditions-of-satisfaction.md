# Conditions of Satisfaction

Before considering a story complete, walk through these conditions.

A story doesn't need to satisfy every point perfectly, but if several answers are "no", there is probably more work to do.

---

# Discovery

☐ Do I understand the real story?

☐ Have I challenged my first interpretation?

☐ Have I explored uncertainty rather than filling the gaps?

☐ Have I asked enough questions?

☐ Can I explain the story in a single sentence?

☐ Do I understand why this story matters?

☐ Am I writing about the real problem rather than the obvious event?

---

# Atmosphere

☐ Can the reader picture where this happened?

☐ Have I included authentic details rather than generic description?

☐ Have I shown behaviour instead of simply naming emotions?

☐ Does the setting support the story?

☐ Have I removed details that don't contribute anything?

---

# Narrative

☐ Is there one dominant message?

☐ Does every section support that message?

☐ Does the story have a natural flow?

☐ Does curiosity pull the reader forward?

☐ Does the ending feel earned rather than announced?

---

# Reflection

☐ Does the story reveal something beyond the events?

☐ Does it challenge assumptions?

☐ Does it explain why the experience mattered?

☐ Does the reader leave with a different understanding than they started with?

☐ Can the reader follow how the narrator's understanding changed?

☐ Have I described the thought process rather than only reporting the lesson?

☐ Does uncertainty appear where the narrator was genuinely uncertain?

☐ Have I allowed the insight to develop gradually?

☐ Have I stayed with the specific experience before broadening its meaning?

---

# Editing

☐ Have I preserved the author's voice?

☐ Have I sharpened rather than rewritten?

☐ Have I removed repetition?

☐ Have I varied sentence length naturally?

☐ Does the story read well aloud?

☐ Does every sentence earn its place?

---

# Anti-Patterns

☐ No manufactured contrast ("It's not X. It's Y.")

☐ No repeated negation ("Not this. Not that. Not because...")

☐ No AI-style rhythm created through repetitive sentence or paragraph structures.

☐ No unnecessary explanation where the reader can infer the meaning.

☐ No invented dialogue or manufactured drama.

☐ No filler.

☐ No conclusions that haven't been earned by the story.

☐ No artificially staged moment of realisation.

☐ No quotation-ready conclusion replacing the narrator's actual thought process.

☐ No moral arranged first and story constructed backwards to support it.

---

# Final Questions

Before publishing, ask yourself:

- What is the one thing I want people to remember six months from now?
- Could any paragraph be removed without changing the message?
- Have I trusted the reader's intelligence?
- Have I written something authentic rather than something that merely sounds profound?
- Would I still be happy to put my name to this in five years' time?

If the answer to the final question is "no", keep writing.
