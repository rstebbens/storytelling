# Reflection

Reflection is where experience becomes understanding.

Events happen.

Stories describe them.

Reflection explains why they mattered.

Without reflection, a story may be entertaining.

With reflection, it becomes useful.

---

# Don't Rush To The Lesson

The first explanation is rarely the deepest one.

Spend time exploring what happened before deciding what it means.

Ask yourself:

- Why did this happen?
- Why did sensible people make those decisions?
- What assumptions existed at the time?
- What wasn't obvious until later?

The goal is understanding, not simply explanation.

---

# Look Beyond The Event

Most events are symptoms.

Reflection looks beneath them.

Ask:

- What was really happening?
- What pressures were influencing people?
- What beliefs shaped their decisions?
- What system produced this outcome?

Understanding often sits below the surface.

---

# Challenge Your Own Thinking

Reflection isn't about proving yourself right.

It's about testing your own interpretation.

Ask:

- What if I'm wrong?
- What evidence would change my mind?
- How might someone else see this?
- What have I assumed without noticing?

Curiosity produces better reflection than certainty.

---

# Understand The People

Every decision makes sense to someone.

Take time to understand why.

Avoid judging people using information they didn't have at the time.

Instead ask:

- What did they believe?
- What were they trying to achieve?
- What constraints existed?
- What trade-offs were they making?

Empathy often reveals what criticism cannot.

---

# Let The Learning Emerge

Don't force a lesson onto the story.

Allow it to emerge naturally.

Sometimes the most valuable insight isn't the one you expected to find.

Follow the evidence rather than the conclusion you hoped to reach.

---

# Find The Universal Truth

The strongest stories move beyond the individual event.

Ask yourself:

- Where else does this happen?
- Why do people keep repeating this pattern?
- What does this reveal about human behaviour?
- What would someone in a completely different profession recognise here?

The broader the insight, the longer the story remains relevant.

---

# Accept Complexity

Not every story has a neat ending.

Sometimes there are competing explanations.

Sometimes several people are right.

Sometimes no solution is perfect.

Reflection should increase understanding, not reduce complexity simply to make the story easier to tell.

---

# End With Perspective

A good reflection leaves the reader thinking differently.

Not because you told them what to think.

Because the story allowed them to discover something for themselves.

The best reflections don't close the conversation.

They open a better one.
