# Editing

Editing is not rewriting.

It is revealing the strongest version of the story that already exists.

The goal is not to sound more impressive.

The goal is to become clearer.

---

# Sharpen, Don't Replace

Respect the author's voice.

Preserve the personality, humour and observations that make the story unique.

Improve clarity without replacing authentic language with generic prose.

Edit with a light touch unless a complete rewrite has been requested.

---

# Every Sentence Should Earn Its Place

Every sentence should contribute something.

It should:

- move the story forward
- deepen understanding
- strengthen atmosphere
- support the message
- create curiosity

If removing a sentence changes nothing, remove it.

---

# Remove What The Reader Already Knows

Readers enjoy making connections.

Don't explain observations they can already infer.

Trust the audience.

Good editing often removes explanation rather than adding it.

---

# Simplify Without Losing Character

Clarity should never come at the expense of personality.

Choose precise words.

Remove unnecessary complexity.

Keep the voice.

---

# Preserve The Rhythm

Read the story aloud.

Natural writing varies its pace.

Some ideas deserve a single sentence.

Others need room to unfold.

The rhythm should feel conversational rather than mechanical.

---

# Strengthen The Structure

Ask:

- Does the beginning create curiosity?
- Does every section support the message?
- Does each paragraph naturally lead to the next?
- Does the ending feel earned?

If not, improve the structure before polishing individual sentences.

---

# Remove Repetition

Ideas often become clearer when repeated less.

Avoid saying the same thing in different ways.

If the reader already understands the point, move on.

---

# Replace The Generic

Whenever possible, replace general statements with specific observations.

Instead of making the writing bigger,

make it more believable.

Specificity creates authenticity.

---

# Read It Like A Stranger

Leave the story for a while.

Return to it as though you've never seen it before.

Ask:

- Where did my attention drift?
- What confused me?
- What surprised me?
- What stayed with me?

The reader experiences the story only once.

Edit from their perspective.

---

# Know When To Stop

Perfect writing doesn't exist.

Eventually, further editing removes personality instead of improving clarity.

Stop when the story feels authentic, clear and complete.

Not when every sentence has been polished into uniformity.
