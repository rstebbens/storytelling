# Narrative

Narrative is the shape of the story.

Discovery finds the story.

Atmosphere gives it somewhere to exist.

Narrative decides how the reader experiences it.

Good stories are not simply collections of facts arranged chronologically.

They reveal information in a way that creates understanding.

---

# Begin In The Right Place

The beginning should create curiosity.

It doesn't need to explain everything.

It should make the reader want to continue.

Sometimes that means beginning before the main event.

Sometimes it means beginning in the middle of it.

Sometimes it means beginning at the end.

Choose the starting point that best serves the story.

---

# Every Story Moves

A story should take the reader somewhere.

Movement might be:

- from certainty to doubt
- from confusion to clarity
- from activity to understanding
- from assumption to evidence
- from individual experience to universal learning

If nothing changes, there may not yet be a story.

---

# Find The Through-Line

Every story needs a thread that runs from beginning to end.

Everything included should strengthen that thread.

If a section doesn't support the story's direction, remove it or tell a different story.

---

# Let Curiosity Lead

Readers naturally ask questions as they read.

Use those questions.

Reveal information when it becomes meaningful rather than as soon as it becomes available.

Curiosity is created by unanswered questions.

Satisfaction comes from answering them at the right moment.

---

# One Story, One Message

Every story should leave the audience with one dominant idea.

Supporting ideas are welcome.

Competing ideas are distracting.

If you can summarise the story in one sentence, the narrative is usually clear.

---

# Earn The Ending

The ending should feel inevitable once it has been reached.

Avoid introducing completely new ideas at the end.

Allow the story to naturally reveal its conclusion.

Readers should feel that they arrived there with you.

---

# Let The Reader Participate

Don't explain every observation.

Don't label every emotion.

Don't interpret every event.

Trust the reader to connect the dots.

Stories become memorable when readers discover part of the meaning themselves.

---

# Pace The Story

Some moments deserve space.

Others deserve momentum.

Slow down where understanding grows.

Move quickly through details that don't.

The pace should reflect the importance of the moment rather than the amount of information available.

---

# Finish With What Changed

A story should leave the reader somewhere different from where they began.

That change may be:

- a new perspective
- a better question
- greater understanding
- a different decision
- a memorable insight

The ending is not simply where the story stops.

It is where the meaning becomes clear.
