# Write A Story

The purpose of this workflow is to tell the strongest version of a story that has already been discovered.

Do not use this prompt to discover the story.

Do not invent missing context.

If the story has not yet been found, route to `discover-the-story.md`.

# Step 1 - Confirm Discovery

Before writing, confirm that you understand:

* What happened.
* What the story is really about.
* What changed.
* Why it matters.
* The single message.
* What the audience should remember.

If any of these are uncertain, stop and ask questions.

Do not compensate with polished prose.

---

# Step 2 - Build The Scene

Anchor the reader.

Help them understand:

* where they are
* who is present
* what the atmosphere feels like
* what details matter

Choose concrete details over generic description.

Small authentic observations usually create stronger imagery than long descriptions.

---

# Step 3 - Tell The Story

Allow the events to unfold naturally.

Avoid rushing towards the lesson.

Show change through actions, conversations and decisions.

Trust the reader to discover meaning.

Do not interrupt the narrative with unnecessary explanation.

---

# Step 4 - Reflect

Allow the narrator's understanding to develop within the story.

Describe what they noticed, what continued to occupy their mind and what only became clear later.

Do not jump directly from the event to a polished conclusion.

Where appropriate, let the narrator question, dismiss, remember and reconsider before understanding what the experience meant.

Reflection should feel like thought unfolding rather than an argument being presented.

Do not explain what the reader already understands.

If the story has already landed emotionally, resist adding another conclusion.

---

# Step 5 - Protect The Voice

Write as though the author is telling the story on their best day.

Do not replace personality with polished corporate writing.

Keep humour, awkwardness, contradictions and imperfections where they make the story feel more human.

Do not optimise for sounding clever.

Optimise for sounding true.

---

# Step 6 - Validate

Before finishing, review the story against the Conditions of Satisfaction.

If an important condition has not been met, improve the story before presenting it.

Do not silently ignore failed conditions.

---

# Step 7 - Final Review

Review the completed story for:

* natural flow
* sentence variety
* pacing
* atmosphere
* clarity
* authenticity
* repetition
* AI anti-patterns

Check whether the story reveals the narrator's thinking or merely reports the lesson they eventually reached.

Look for sentences that package the meaning too neatly.

Where the prose feels assembled, return to the remembered moment, observation or thought that produced the insight.

Remove anything that distracts from the story.

Do not remove details that make the story memorable.

---

# Final Principle

The objective is not to write beautiful prose.

The objective is to tell a story that someone will still remember long after they have forgotten the individual sentences.
