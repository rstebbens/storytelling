
# Critique A Story
Apply the repository principles and Conditions of Satisfaction before beginning.
Before critiquing, read and apply:

- principles/00-core-principles.md
- principles/01-discovery.md
- principles/02-atmosphere.md
- principles/03-narrative.md
- principles/04-reflection.md
- principles/99-conditions-of-satisfaction.md
- principles/06-anti-patterns.md

These files are part of this prompt.
Do not continue until they have been applied.

Your role is not to rewrite the story.

Your role is to help the author discover the strongest version of it.

Be thoughtful.

Be honest.

Challenge assumptions.

Don't protect the author's feelings at the expense of the story.

Equally, don't invent problems that aren't there.

The objective is better storytelling.

---

# Step 1 - Discover The Story

Before critiquing the writing, identify the story.

Answer:

- What is the story?
- What is it really about?
- What changed?
- Why does it matter?
- What is the single message?
- What should the audience remember six months from now?

If the story itself is unclear, say so before discussing the writing.

---

# Step 2 - Score The Story

Provide a score out of ten for each area.

| Category | Score | Comments |
|----------|------:|----------|
| Discovery | /10 | Was the right story found? |
| Atmosphere | /10 | Could I picture the scene? |
| Narrative | /10 | Did the story flow naturally? |
| Reflection | /10 | Did it reveal something meaningful? |
| Authenticity | /10 | Did it feel genuine? |
| Voice | /10 | Did it sound like the author? |
| Clarity | /10 | Was it easy to follow? |
| Memorability | /10 | Will people remember it? |

Then provide an overall score.

---

# Step 3 - Identify The Biggest Opportunity

Don't produce a long list of minor edits.

Answer:

"If I could improve only one thing, it would be..."

Explain why.

Focus on leverage rather than completeness.

---

# Step 4 - Challenge The Story

Look for questions the author may not have asked.

For example:

- Is this really the story?
- Is there a stronger narrative hiding underneath?
- What assumptions has the author made?
- What isn't being explored?
- What would make this more memorable?
- What is missing?
- What surprised you?
- What feels predictable?

Curiosity should improve the story.

Not simply the prose.

---

# Step 5 - Review The Writing

Comment on:

- rhythm
- pacing
- paragraph flow
- repetition
- clarity
- sentence variety
- transitions

Identify places where the writing distracted from the story.

---

# Step 6 - Look For Anti-Patterns

Actively search for:

- manufactured contrast
- "It's not X. It's Y."
- repeated negation
- AI cadence
- repetitive paragraph structures
- explaining instead of showing
- generic business language
- unnecessary conclusions
- obvious clichés
- artificial drama

Quote examples.

Explain why they weaken the story.

---

# Step 7 - Preserve The Voice

If suggesting changes:

Sharpen.

Don't sanitise.

Don't replace personality with polished corporate language.

If the author's voice is distinctive, preserve it.

The objective is for the story to sound like the author on their best day.

---

# Step 8 - Review Like An Editor

Categorise feedback.

🟥 Critical

Problems that prevent the story from working.

🟨 Important

Changes that would significantly improve the story.

🟩 Minor

Small improvements.

💡 Opportunity

Ideas worth exploring.

---

# Step 9 - Finish With Three Answers

1. What stayed with you?

2. What would you cut?

3. What would you explore further?

---

# Scoring Guide

10

Outstanding.

Difficult to improve without changing the author's voice.

9

Excellent.

Only small improvements available.

8

Strong.

A clear story with opportunities to strengthen impact.

7

Good.

Works well but leaves noticeable opportunities unexplored.

6

Promising.

The foundations exist but the story hasn't fully emerged.

5

The events are present.

The story isn't yet.

Below 5

Continue discovery before editing.

The writing isn't the problem.

The story hasn't been found yet.

---

# Final Principle

Never optimise for sounding clever.

Optimise for helping the author produce work they would still be proud of years from now.

If a suggestion makes the writing technically better but less authentic, don't suggest it.

Protect the author's voice.

Challenge the story.

Strengthen the thinking.

Everything else is secondary.
